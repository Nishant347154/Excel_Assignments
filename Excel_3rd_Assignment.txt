1. How and when to use the AutoSum command in excel?
---> AutoSum command in Excel to quickly calculate the sum of a range of numbers. It's handy when you want to add up a
  series of values without typing out the formula manually.
To use AutoSum:
 .Select the cell where you want the sum to appear.
 .Click on the AutoSum button in the Editing group on the Home tab.
 .Excel will automatically suggest a range for the sum based on nearby numbers. You can adjust the range if needed.
 .Press Enter to apply the sum.
 .AutoSum is particularly useful when you have a column or row of numbers that you want to add up quickly without writing
  a formula.


2. What is the shortcut key to perform AutoSum?
---> Alt + = will automatically insert the SUM function and suggest a range of adjacent cells for the sum. You can then
  press Enter to apply the sum.


3. How do you get rid of Formula that omits adjacent cells?
---> To get rid of a formula that omits adjacent cells in Excel:
.Select the cell containing the formula.
.Press the Delete key on your keyboard.
.This will remove the formula from the selected cell, allowing you to start fresh or input a new formula.


4. How do you select non-adjacent cells in Excel 2016?
---> To select non-adjacent cells in Excel 2016:
 .Click on the first cell you want to select.
 .Hold down the Ctrl key on your keyboard.
 .While holding Ctrl, click on each additional cell you want to include in the selection.
 This will allow you to select multiple cells that are not next to each other.


5. What happens if you choose a column, hold down the Alt key and press the letters
ocw in quick succession?
---> In Excel, if you choose a column, hold down the Alt key, and press the letters "ocw" in quick succession, it 
 automatically resizes the column to fit the widest cell content in that column. This keyboard shortcut is a quick way 
 to adjust the width of a column to best fit its contents.


6. If you right-click on a row reference number and click on Insert, where will the row
be added?
---> If you right-click on a row reference number (e.g., "1", "2", "3", etc.) in Excel and click on "Insert", 
the new row will be inserted above the row you right-clicked on. In other words, the row will be added immediately above
 the selected row reference number.
