1. What do you mean by AutoComplete feature in Excel and what are the
benefits of using this feature?
---> The AutoComplete feature in Excel predicts and suggests entries based on previously entered values or patterns.
    Benefits include: 
. Faster data entry by reducing manual typing.
. Accuracy improvements by minimizing typographical errors.
. Efficiency gains when working with repetitive data sets.
. Enhanced productivity due to reduced need for manual data input.


2. Explain working with workbooks and working with cells.
---> Working with workbooks involves tasks such as creating, opening, saving, and closing Excel files. 
 You can organize your data into different workbooks for better management and organization. Working with cells 
 involves tasks such as entering data, formatting cells, applying formulas and functions, and navigating within the 
 worksheet. You can select, copy, paste, and edit cell contents to manipulate data as needed. Additionally, you can 
 apply various formatting options to cells to enhance the presentation and readability of your data.


3. What is fill handle in Excel and why do we use it?
--->The fill handle in Excel is a small square at the bottom-right corner of a selected cell or range. 
 It allows users to quickly fill adjacent cells with data or formulas based on the existing content. We use it to save 
 time and effort by automatically copying and filling data or formulas into adjacent cells, thus avoiding manual entry 
 for repetitive patterns or sequences.


4. Give some examples of using the fill handle.
--->
. Copying Values: Enter a value into a cell, then click and drag the fill handle across adjacent cells to copy the value 
 into those cells.

. Filling Series: Enter a value into a cell, then click and drag the fill handle to create a series
  (e.g., numbers, dates, months) in adjacent cells.

. Copying Formulas: Enter a formula into a cell, then click and drag the fill handle across adjacent cells to copy 
 the formula and automatically adjust cell references.



5. Describe flash fill and what the different ways to access the flash fill are.
---> Flash Fill is a feature in Excel that automatically fills in values based on patterns it recognizes in adjacent cells.
    It's useful for quickly formatting data without having to write complex formulas.
 Different ways to access Flash Fill:
. Using the Ribbon: Go to the "Data" tab on the ribbon, then click on "Flash Fill" in the "Data Tools" group.
. Keyboard Shortcut: Press "Ctrl + E" to activate Flash Fill.
. Auto Fill Options: After manually filling in a pattern, a small square box appears in the bottom-right corner of the
  selected cell. Clicking on this box gives options to apply Flash Fill.
. Right-Click Menu: Right-click on the cell where you want to apply Flash Fill, then select "Flash Fill" from the context 
  menu.
